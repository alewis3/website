Slips Review:

A slip is defined as a person intending to do the appropriate action, but instead ending up doing something else. Norman describes this as follows: "when the action performed is not the same as the action that was intended," (171). There are two further classifications of slips:

  • Action-Based

    In action-based slips, the wrong action is performed. This can mean performing the wrong action on the correct object, or performing the correct action on the wrong object.

  • Memory-Lapse

    In memory-lapse slips, the intended action is not done, or simply forgotten about, usually due to an interruption between the time an action is decided upon and the time it is complete.

Action slips can further be divided into three categoriesns:

  • Capture slips

    Capture slips are when you are intending to do an action, and instead a more familiar action occurs; it captures the action.

  • Description-similarity slips

    In description-similarity slips, an item similar to the target item is acted upon.

  • Mode error

    Mode error slips occur when the controls of an object mean different things in different modes.
